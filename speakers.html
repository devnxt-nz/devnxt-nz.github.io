Coraline Ada Ehmke

A 25-year veteran of software engineering, Coraline Ada Ehmke has worked for over a decade at the intersection of open source and social justice. She is the creator of Contributor Covenant, the first and most popular code of conduct for open source communities, and the Hippocratic License, an innovative ethical open source license designed to promote and protect human rights. Coraline co-founded the Organization for Ethical Source and currently serves as its Executive Director.

My passion is to bring workplace happiness out of the clouds and into reality by enabling workplaces to become happy and productive environments where talented people come together to do what they do best. This passion arose after seeing the good, the bad, and the ugly sides of corporate office environments, and from my own eclectic experiences arising from over 20 years in the corporate property industry.

I’m determined that workplace happiness need not be a myth, and so my journey involves influencing employers and employees through the creation of online courses, speaking engagements, and consulting to C-suite executives on the importance of workplace happiness and its link to productivity and the bottom line.

Jess Dodson

Chances are if you’ve run into “GirlGerms” online, you’ve spoken to Jess! Based in Brisbane Australia, Jess joined Microsoft in 2019 and is now a Senior Cloud Solution Architect in Cyber Security, after working in a mixture of both government and higher education industries for over 15 years.

Jess’s role is both deeply technical and deeply customer focused, providing technical leadership for customers within ANZ to help secure their IT environments using Microsoft products, tools, and technologies. Jess is keen to help other women break into the industry – both tech generally and cyber security specifically – and helps co-run the Women IT Pros ANZ call each month; as well as speaking at conferences, talking on podcasts and interviews, and writing blog posts around how we can make this industry more inclusive for women. Jess is an advocate for authenticity – being yourself is so important and feeling comfortable to be yourself at work can allow you to shine even brighter.

Josh Robb

Josh has over 20 years’ experience leading teams and building software products in Australasia, Europe and the USA. He has built products in verticals including eLearning, advertising, human resources, FinTech and now HealthTech. Josh led the growth of Pushpay’s product design and development for 6 years from 4 to over 100 people and $200m of annual revenue. He co-founded Tend Health in 2019 with Cecilia and James Robinson and Dr Mataroria Lyndon where he’s working on completely reimagining the traditional doctors visit.
